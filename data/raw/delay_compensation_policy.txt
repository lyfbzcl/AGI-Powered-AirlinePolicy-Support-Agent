Delay Compensation Policy

This policy describes the assistance, reimbursement, and compensation options that may apply when a flight is delayed. The support available to passengers depends on the length of the delay, the route involved, the reason for the disruption, the applicable law, and whether the flight is operated by the airline or by a partner carrier. Not every delay qualifies for compensation. In many cases, the airline's obligation is limited to providing updates, reasonable care during the disruption, and rebooking assistance rather than a cash payment.

Flight status updates:
When a delay is identified, the airline will generally communicate revised departure information by airport display screens, boarding announcements, email, SMS, or the mobile app using the contact details attached to the booking. Passengers are responsible for ensuring their contact information is accurate and for monitoring airport announcements during travel. Published departure times may continue to change as operational conditions evolve, and an estimated time of departure should not be interpreted as a guarantee. Even when notifications are sent, passengers should remain at the gate area or follow airport instructions because boarding can begin on short notice once the aircraft is ready.

Minor delays:
For shorter delays, the airline will typically provide operational updates and, where possible, revised boarding times. Minor delays do not automatically trigger compensation, reimbursement, or access to meals, hotel accommodation, or ground transport. Passengers should avoid purchasing replacement tickets or additional travel services unless instructed to do so by the airline, because voluntary purchases made without authorization may not be reimbursed.

Extended delays and passenger care:
If a delay becomes significant, the airline may provide reasonable care based on the circumstances, local law, and availability at the airport. Reasonable care may include meal vouchers, refreshments, communication assistance, hotel accommodation for an overnight delay, or transport between the airport and an approved hotel. Such support is generally provided only when the delay is within the airline's control or when required by law. Hotel rooms are subject to availability and may be limited near the airport during severe weather or broad operational disruptions. Passengers who choose to arrange their own hotel or meals without prior approval may be reimbursed only for reasonable documented expenses and only when the policy or law allows it.

Rebooking and rerouting:
When a delay causes a misconnection or materially disrupts the passenger's itinerary, the airline may rebook the passenger on the next available flight operated by the airline, or in some cases on a partner airline or an alternative routing. If the passenger accepts an alternative flight, cash compensation rights may be reduced or removed depending on the applicable law and the delay eventually experienced at the final destination. If no acceptable alternative is available, eligible passengers may request a refund of the unused portion of the ticket under the refund policy. Rebooking options may be limited by seat availability, immigration restrictions, airport curfews, or airport security requirements.

Compensation eligibility:
Cash or fixed compensation is not automatically available for every delay. Eligibility generally depends on whether the disruption was caused by factors within the airline's control, the length of the final arrival delay, and the passenger's compliance with check-in and boarding requirements. Delays caused by severe weather, air traffic control restrictions, airport security incidents, political instability, labor action outside the airline's control, public health measures, or safety-related events may not qualify for monetary compensation even when the delay is lengthy. Delays caused by operational issues within the airline's control may qualify for compensation only on routes or in markets where a law, regulation, or published customer commitment provides such payment.

Claim requirements:
Passengers seeking reimbursement or compensation should retain boarding passes, booking confirmations, baggage receipts, and itemized receipts for any approved out-of-pocket expenses. Claims may need to be submitted within a specified time after travel, and incomplete claims may be delayed or rejected. The airline may request additional information to verify the cause of the disruption, the amount claimed, proof of travel, and proof that the expense was necessary and reasonable. Expenses relating to alcohol, luxury accommodation, upgraded transportation, or speculative onward losses are usually not reimbursable unless required by law.

Limitations and exclusions:
The airline is not responsible for indirect or consequential losses arising from a delayed flight except where applicable law provides otherwise. This includes missed business opportunities, lost wages, missed events, separate ticket failures, or expenses caused by the passenger's independent arrangements. If the passenger chooses not to travel after a delay but declines the rebooking options or assistance offered, compensation and reimbursement may be limited. Any compensation already paid under one regime may be deducted from amounts owed under another regime to prevent duplicate recovery.

Important note:
Passengers should present themselves for check-in and boarding on time even when a delay has been announced, because eligibility for support and compensation may be lost if the passenger fails to comply with operational instructions. Keeping travel documents and receipts will help the airline review any later claim.
