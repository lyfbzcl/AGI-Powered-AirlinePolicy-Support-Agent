Flight Change Policy

This flight change policy explains when a passenger may change the date, time, or routing of an existing booking. Flight changes are subject to fare conditions, seat availability in the applicable booking class, operational limits, and payment of any difference between the original fare and the new fare selected. Some tickets permit changes with low or no service fee, while others do not permit voluntary changes at all. The fare rules attached to the ticket remain the controlling terms for any requested change.

Eligibility to change a booking:
Passengers may request a change before the scheduled departure time of the first unused flight coupon unless a different deadline is stated in the fare rules. Requests made after departure are generally not permitted except on fares specifically marked as changeable after departure. A change request is valid only when the passenger has received confirmation and any required additional payment has been collected. Starting a change request online without completing payment or confirmation does not preserve the original fare or prevent the ticket from becoming a no-show if the original flight departs.

Advance flight changes:
For changes made before the day of departure, the passenger may need to pay both a change fee and any fare difference between the original ticket and the newly selected itinerary. If the new flight is cheaper, the treatment of the remaining value depends on the fare rules. Some fares preserve a residual credit, while others do not refund any difference. Changes may also require the ticket to be reissued in the same cabin or fare family, and promotional inventory may not be available on the new date. If the new itinerary involves different airports, a longer route, or a different carrier, additional taxes, airport charges, or partner airline fees may apply.

Same-day changes:
Passengers traveling on eligible fares may request a same-day confirmed change to an earlier or later flight on the same calendar day as the original departure. Same-day changes are usually limited to the same origin and destination, the same passenger, and the same operating carrier. A published same-day change fee may apply unless waived by status benefits or premium fare conditions. Some passengers may instead be offered same-day standby, which does not guarantee a seat and is processed according to airport and operational procedures. Same-day changes are subject to seat availability and may be restricted for international sectors, flights involving security screening deadlines, or itineraries that include checked baggage already accepted on the original flight.

Restricted fares and exceptions:
Basic economy, promotional sale fares, group fares, charter tickets, and some tickets issued by travel agents may not permit voluntary changes. In those cases, the passenger may need to cancel the booking under the applicable cancellation rules and purchase a new ticket if permitted. Certain compassionate or disruption-related exceptions may be offered when a change is requested because of medical emergency, bereavement, or official travel restrictions, but documentation may be required and approval is not automatic. The airline may also refuse a change if the passenger has not met payment conditions, ticket validity rules, or visa and entry requirements for the new itinerary.

Changes to connecting itineraries:
When a booking contains multiple sectors on the same ticket, changing one segment may require the entire itinerary to be repriced. This can affect both the fare owed and the baggage allowance attached to the ticket. If a passenger asks to remove a connecting sector or stop over in a city not originally permitted by the fare, the new itinerary may be priced as a completely separate journey. Changes involving partner-operated or codeshare flights may require manual review and can take longer to confirm. Some partner fares do not allow partial changes once travel has begun.

Ancillary services and seat assignments:
Additional services purchased for the original flight, including checked baggage, seat selection, lounge access, onboard Wi-Fi, or special meals, may not automatically transfer to the new booking. Where possible, the airline will transfer the service or provide comparable accommodation, but some services may need to be reselected or repurchased. If the passenger fails to request a transfer before departure, unused optional services may become non-refundable according to their own terms. Passengers needing wheelchair assistance, unaccompanied minor service, pet travel, or medical clearance should contact the airline promptly after making a change so that the new flight can be reviewed for suitability.

Passenger responsibilities:
Passengers are responsible for checking that the revised itinerary, fare collection, seat assignment, and baggage allowance are correct after a change is processed. Change confirmations should be reviewed immediately, especially when the new itinerary includes overnight connections, airport changes within the same city, or separate tickets. The passenger remains responsible for meeting all check-in deadlines for the new flight and for ensuring that passports, visas, transit permissions, and health documents remain valid for the revised travel dates.

Important note:
All voluntary flight changes must be finalized before the relevant deadline in the fare rules. A request is not complete until the airline has confirmed the new itinerary and collected any applicable fee or fare difference.
