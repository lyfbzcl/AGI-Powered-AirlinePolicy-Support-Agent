Check-In Policy

This check-in policy explains the deadlines and passenger responsibilities that apply before boarding. Check-in is the process by which the airline verifies the passenger's reservation, travel documents, baggage, and readiness to travel. Completing check-in on time is essential. A confirmed reservation does not guarantee travel if the passenger fails to meet check-in, baggage drop, document verification, security, or boarding deadlines. Different airports and routes may have different cutoff times, and the times shown in the booking confirmation or on the airline website are the deadlines that apply to the specific flight.

Online and mobile check-in:
Online and mobile check-in typically open 24 hours before scheduled departure, although some routes may open earlier or later due to airport, government, or document requirements. On eligible itineraries, passengers may select seats, confirm contact details, and receive a mobile or printable boarding pass during this period. Online check-in may not be available if document checks are required at the airport, if the booking involves an unaccompanied minor, pet travel, extra seat purchase, special medical clearance, or certain international destinations with visa verification requirements. A passenger who checks in online must still comply with baggage drop and boarding deadlines. Checking in online does not remove the need to present required documents on request.

Airport check-in and baggage drop:
Passengers who need to check baggage, complete document verification, or receive special assistance should arrive at the airport with enough time to complete all formalities. Airport check-in counters and bag drop facilities may open and close at fixed times based on local operating conditions. In general, domestic services may require check-in and bag drop to be completed at least 45 to 60 minutes before departure, while international services may require completion 60 to 90 minutes before departure or earlier on certain routes. Some airports operate self-service kiosks or dedicated bag drop counters, but availability of these facilities does not extend the published deadline. Baggage presented after the cutoff time may be refused even if the passenger has already checked in online.

Document requirements:
Passengers are solely responsible for carrying valid passports, visas, residence permits, health certificates, and any other documents required by the departure, transit, and destination authorities. The airline may refuse check-in or boarding if documents are missing, inconsistent with the booking, damaged, or invalid for travel. The airline may also request proof of onward travel, local contact details, or additional entry documentation where required by government authorities. A boarding pass issued online or at a kiosk remains subject to cancellation if document inspection later shows that the passenger does not meet travel requirements.

Seat assignment and standby:
Seat assignments issued during check-in remain subject to operational change, aircraft substitution, safety requirements, and rebalancing of the aircraft cabin. Passengers who fail to present themselves at the boarding gate by the required time may lose their reserved seat even if they completed check-in on time. In overbooking or disruption situations, the airline may manage standby and seat allocation according to operational needs, status benefits, fare rules, and applicable denied boarding procedures. Completing check-in early does not guarantee protection from all seat changes, but late check-in increases the risk that preferred seating or even travel on the booked flight may no longer be available.

Late arrival and refusal of travel:
If a passenger arrives after the published check-in deadline, the airline may refuse travel on that flight and treat the booking as a no-show according to the fare rules. The same outcome may apply when the passenger arrives at security or the boarding gate too late, even if check-in was completed earlier. The airline is not responsible for delays caused by road traffic, parking shortages, long airport queues, or late arrival at the wrong terminal. Passengers should allow additional time during school holidays, severe weather, airport construction, or heightened security conditions.

Special cases:
Passengers requiring wheelchair assistance, carriage of mobility aids, pet travel, unaccompanied minor handling, infant travel documents, carriage of firearms where permitted, or transport of oversized or special baggage should contact the airline in advance and arrive earlier than standard recommendation. Some requests must be approved before airport check-in can be completed. Passengers traveling on separate tickets are responsible for confirming whether baggage can be through-checked and whether additional document or immigration clearance will be required between flights.

Passenger responsibilities:
Passengers must verify their flight time before leaving for the airport because schedule changes, terminal changes, and gate changes can occur. They should ensure that names on the booking match travel documents, that baggage complies with published rules, and that contact details remain current. After check-in, passengers must proceed through security and be present at the gate no later than the boarding time shown on the boarding pass or announced by staff. Failure to comply with staff instructions, security screening requirements, or boarding deadlines may result in denied travel without refund beyond what is allowed in the fare conditions.

Important note:
Published check-in deadlines are strict operational deadlines, not recommended arrival times. Passengers should arrive well before the cutoff and allow enough time for queues, document checks, baggage processing, security screening, and walking time to the gate.
