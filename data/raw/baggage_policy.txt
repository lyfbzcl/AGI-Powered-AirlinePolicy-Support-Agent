Baggage Policy

This baggage policy explains the standard rules that apply to carry-on baggage, checked baggage, excess baggage, and special items carried on flights operated by the airline. Baggage allowance may vary by fare type, route, cabin purchased, frequent flyer tier, and any local legal requirements that apply to the journey. The allowance shown on the passenger's booking confirmation or electronic ticket is the controlling allowance for travel. Passengers are responsible for reviewing the allowance for each flight in their itinerary before arriving at the airport, especially when the itinerary includes separate tickets, codeshare flights, or travel on partner airlines.

Carry-on baggage:
Most passengers may bring one standard carry-on bag and one small personal item into the cabin. A personal item should fit under the seat in front of the passenger and may include a handbag, laptop bag, briefcase, or small backpack. The maximum size for standard carry-on baggage is 56 cm x 36 cm x 23 cm, including wheels, side pockets, and handles. Carry-on baggage must be light enough for the passenger to lift and store without assistance unless assistance is required because of a disability. If overhead bin space becomes limited, the airline may require some carry-on baggage to be checked at the gate without prior notice. Cabin baggage that exceeds the applicable size or weight limit may be refused at the boarding gate and checked subject to any applicable fee.

Checked baggage:
On many economy fares, one checked bag up to 23 kg may be included, while premium cabins or higher-status passengers may receive a larger allowance. Some basic or promotional fares do not include checked baggage and require a separate purchase. Each checked bag must also comply with the airline's maximum linear dimension limit, usually measured by adding length, width, and height. Bags that exceed size or weight limits may incur oversize or overweight fees in addition to any charge for extra bags. For safety reasons, the airline will not accept a single checked item weighing more than 32 kg. Passengers with heavier items must repack the contents into separate bags before check-in.

Additional, overweight, and oversize baggage:
Fees apply when a passenger checks more bags than the allowance included in the ticket, or when a checked bag exceeds the standard size or weight limit. Additional bag charges, overweight charges, and oversize charges are cumulative unless local law requires otherwise. Prepaid baggage rates purchased online before airport check-in are usually lower than airport rates. Published excess baggage charges may vary by route, currency, and season, and are subject to change until ticketing. Excess baggage is accepted only when space and weight limits on the flight permit. During peak travel periods, weather disruptions, or aircraft changes, the airline may limit the number of extra bags accepted on a flight.

Special items:
Sports equipment, musical instruments, medical equipment, strollers, child restraint systems, and certain mobility aids may be accepted under separate conditions. Some special items count toward the standard checked baggage allowance, while others are carried free of charge when required for accessibility or infant travel. Large sporting items, such as surfboards, bicycles, skis, or golf equipment, may require advance notice and may be subject to dedicated handling fees. Musical instruments that fit safely in the cabin may be carried as carry-on baggage if they meet cabin rules. Larger instruments may require the purchase of an additional seat or transport as checked baggage in an approved protective case.

Restricted and prohibited items:
Passengers must not place dangerous goods, prohibited articles, or items restricted by aviation security rules in checked or carry-on baggage except as specifically permitted. Spare lithium batteries, power banks, electronic cigarettes, and certain medical devices are subject to special carriage restrictions. Fragile, perishable, or high-value items such as jewelry, cash, negotiable documents, and important medication should be carried in the cabin and remain the passenger's responsibility. The airline may refuse baggage that is improperly packed, leaks, emits odor, appears unsafe to handle, or could damage other baggage or the aircraft.

Passenger responsibilities:
Passengers must ensure that all baggage is packed securely, clearly labeled with current contact information, and presented for check-in before the baggage acceptance deadline. Any bag accepted without a lock remains subject to security inspection by airport or government authorities. Where a lock is used, passengers should use an approved lock type where required and understand that security authorities may open the bag for inspection. Baggage checked on one flight may not automatically be through-checked to a final destination when travel includes separate tickets, overnight stops, customs re-clearance, or partner carriers with different rules. Passengers are responsible for collecting and rechecking baggage whenever instructed to do so.

Infants, children, and special assistance:
Passengers traveling with an infant may usually check a stroller or collapsible pushchair and, on eligible fares, one additional infant item such as a car seat. Mobility aids used by passengers with disabilities are carried according to applicable accessibility obligations and safety requirements. The airline may ask for battery information, dimensions, or handling instructions in advance for electric wheelchairs or similar devices. Passengers who require special baggage handling or extra time at the airport should notify the airline as early as possible before departure.

Delayed, damaged, or missing baggage:
If checked baggage is delayed, damaged, or missing on arrival, the passenger should report the issue to the airline's baggage service desk before leaving the airport whenever possible. A written report may be required within the time limit specified by applicable conventions or local law. Passengers should retain baggage tags, boarding passes, receipts, and photographs of any damage. Reimbursement for essential purchases during a baggage delay may be limited to reasonable and documented expenses. The airline's liability for lost, delayed, or damaged baggage is limited by applicable law and international conventions unless a higher declared value has been accepted where available.

Important note:
Baggage rules can differ by route, fare family, and operating carrier. Passengers should review the baggage allowance on their booking confirmation before travel and allow extra time for payment, inspection, or repacking if traveling with additional or special baggage.
